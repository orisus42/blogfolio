## Presentations and Awards

[This repository](https://github.com/orisus42/delaunay-triangulation) contains my slides for my first summer research internship, at my university under a faculty mentor. I explored the computational problem of [Delaunay Triangulation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Delaunay_triangulation) that arrives in certain computer graphics methods involving mapping of surfaces and altitude curvatures (for landscape generation) and creating convex sets in geometry. As this was my first ever undergraduate research project, it **may contain errors or inaccuracies**.

[This project](https://github.com/orisus42/cpu-sim-8bit) (including a report) was presented to the course instructor in my "**Computer Organization & Architecture**" lab with my team of three other students. It contains a custom ISA which was built to run the [Fibonacci sequence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fibonacci_sequence) program on an **8-bit CPU simulation** on [TkGate](https://github.com/bnoordhuis/tkgate). I am currently working to translate this project to a full 32-bit RISC-V standard ISA with simulation work on Vivado using SystemVerilog. More about this project in the projects tab.

I presented [this research poster](https://github.com/orisus42/indo-quantum-summit-poster) at the **Indo-Quantum Summit 2026**, held at the [Pandit Deendayal Energy University](https://pdeu.ac.in), Gujarat. This was a national level quantum computing summit where many leading researchers and industrial minds were invited to discuss India's potential in the field of QC. My poster was one of the four posters that **got awarded with the best poster award**! The work contains my study on a new type of circuits in QC, called "**Peaked Circuits**".

I won the second place prize at [IIITH]()'s hackathon held in collaboration with **IBM Qiskit Fall Fest'25**, where we had to build a quantum version of the [Battleship](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battleship_(puzzle)). Although the time frame was small (I started like 6 hours late lol) I managed to build a small report of a simple analysis of the board. [This repository](https://github.com/orisus42/isaqc-qff-battleship) contains the material and the code. It was forked from the original problem statement.

## Work

During my fourth semester at university, I got to work at [Zenith Studio](https://www.quantzen.live) (or QuantZen) as a **PQC Analyst Intern** with their project on building a post-quantum secure mail wrapper framework that uses PQC encryption algorithms like [ML-KEM](https://nvlpubs.nist.gov/nistpubs/fips/nist.fips.203.pdf) and [ML-DSA](https://nvlpubs.nist.gov/nistpubs/fips/nist.fips.204.pdf) to encrypt mails end-to-end for businesses and clients. This protects against the threat model of "harvest now, decrypt later" (learn about this threat model [here](https://www.paloaltonetworks.com/cyberpedia/harvest-now-decrypt-later-hndl)) and provides a secure encryption pipeline directly at the client side. Currently this project, **"QuantMail"** is under development, and will be released for existing SMTP providers like Gmail and Outlook.
